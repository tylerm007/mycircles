Three Circles

Recovery Through Understanding & Structure

●

Inner Circle

The Inner Circle represents your core destructive behaviors and addictive patterns that you're committed to avoiding completely. These are the "bottom line" behaviors that create the most significant harm in your life and relationships.

By clearly defining what belongs in your Inner Circle, you create firm boundaries around the most dangerous behaviors. This circle serves as your ultimate accountability measure - crossing into these behaviors signals immediate need for support and intervention.

◐

Middle Circle

The Middle Circle contains behaviors that aren't immediately destructive but can lead you toward your Inner Circle. These are warning signs, risky situations, and behaviors that compromise your recovery goals without being bottom-line violations.

Middle Circle behaviors require careful attention and mindful decision-making. While not forbidden, they serve as important indicators that you may be moving in a dangerous direction. Recognizing these patterns helps you course-correct before reaching critical moments.

○

Outer Circle

The Outer Circle encompasses all the positive, healthy behaviors and activities that support your recovery and overall wellbeing. These are the constructive choices that move you toward your goals and values.

Outer Circle behaviors include healthy relationships, meaningful work, physical wellness, spiritual practices, and activities that align with your authentic self. The more time and energy you invest in Outer Circle behaviors, the stronger your recovery foundation becomes.

○

Daily Activities

The Daily Activities Circle encompasses all the positive, healthy behaviors and activities that support your recovery and overall wellbeing. These are the constructive choices that move you toward your goals and values.

Daily Activities include healthy relationships, meaningful work, physical wellness, spiritual practices, and activities that align with your authentic self. The more time and energy you invest in Daily Activities, the stronger your recovery foundation becomes.
